## Technical Ownership

I was responsible for maintaining and explaining the documentation that demonstrates the project's development history. This includes:

* Project overview and objectives
* System architecture documentation
* Sprint packets and progress reports
* Quality assurance (QA) records
* AI usage and audit documentation
* Final portfolio and project evidence

My role ensured that project activities could be traced, verified, and understood by instructors, teammates, and external reviewers.

---

## Challenge and Resolution

### Potential Risk

A major risk was that important work could be overlooked if evidence was not properly linked to supporting documentation.

### Solution

To address this, I ensured that significant project claims were connected to relevant files, GitHub issues, pull requests, screenshots, reports, or demonstration materials. This improved transparency and strengthened the credibility of the project.

---

## Use of AI Tools

AI tools were used to assist with organizing documentation, improving writing quality, and structuring portfolio content. However, I remained responsible for verifying all information, ensuring that references were accurate, and confirming that the documented project history reflected actual team activities.

## Reflection

This project taught me that documentation is a critical part of software engineering, not just an administrative task. Well-organized documentation helps others understand the project's goals, development process, and outcomes while building trust in the team's work.

I am proud of contributing to the clarity and professionalism of the project through effective documentation and evidence management. Looking back, I could have improved by updating evidence links more consistently during each sprint rather than organizing them later. In future projects, I plan to maintain a structured evidence checklist throughout the development cycle to improve efficiency and accuracy.
