The project demonstration could be affected if the deployment environment, server configuration, or database connection is unavailable during presentation. To minimize this risk, the team prepared contingency measures including:

* Running the application through a local Tomcat server.
* Maintaining updated screenshots of completed functionality.
* Documenting known limitations and alternative demonstration approaches.
* Keeping testing evidence available to support system validation.

## Reflection

This project taught me that quality assurance extends beyond finding bugs. Effective QA helps reduce project risks, improve user confidence, and ensure successful presentations. Through testing activities, I gained experience in identifying defects, documenting issues, validating system functionality, and communicating findings to the development team.

One of my most valuable contributions was making project limitations visible and ensuring the team was aware of potential risks before the final demonstration. This helped improve project transparency and preparedness.

Looking back, I would have benefited from capturing testing evidence and screenshots earlier in the development process. More comprehensive documentation would have provided stronger validation records and improved traceability.

For future projects, I plan to implement repeatable automated smoke tests and adopt more structured testing methodologies to improve efficiency and software quality.
